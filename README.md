# HR--Analytics--Dashboard
The HR Analytics Dashboard is an interactive data visualization project designed to analyze employee data and uncover key insights related to attrition, workforce demographics, and job satisfaction. It provides a comprehensive overview of employee trends, helping organizations identify the factors contributing to employee turnover.


## Purpose of the Dashboard
To monitor employee attrition trends
To identify problem areas in the organization
To support strategic HR decision-making
To improve employee satisfaction and retention


## Dashboard Breakdown
## 1. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
Total Employees (1,470): Overall workforce size
Attrition Count (237): Number of employees who left
Attrition Rate (16.12%): Percentage of employees who left
Active Employees (1,233): Current workforce
Average Age (37): Average age of employees
These KPIs give a high-level understanding of organizational health.

 ## 2. Attrition by Gender
 Helps identify gender-based attrition trends.

## 3. Department-wise Attrition
Visualized using a pie chart
R&D has the highest attrition, followed by Sales, while HR has the lowest
 Helps HR focus on departments with high turnover.

## 4. Employee Distribution by Age Group
Bar chart showing number of employees across age groups
Majority fall between 30–40 years
Useful for workforce planning and demographic analysis.

## 5. Job Satisfaction Rating
Table showing satisfaction levels (1–4) across different job roles
Provides insight into employee engagement per role
Helps identify roles with low satisfaction that may lead to attrition.

## 6. Education Field-wise Attrition
Bar chart showing attrition based on education background
Highest attrition in Life Sciences and Medical fields
Shows how education impacts employee retention.

## 7. Attrition by Age Group & Gender
Donut charts representing attrition distribution across age groups and gender
Highest attrition in 25–34 age group
Indicates early-career employees are more likely to leave.

## The dashboard reveals that:

Attrition is higher in specific departments (R&D, Sales)
Mid-age employees (25–34) are more likely to leave
Job satisfaction varies by role, impacting retention
Education and gender also influence attrition patterns**
